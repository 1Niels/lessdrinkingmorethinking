Description:

      Tequila is a distilled liquor usually unage, and produced by the fermented juice of the Mexican Agave tequilana Weber plant. Tequila comes in an abundant array of colors that ranges from a simple clear distilled beverage to a dark amber brown. The color of the beverage varies greatly on the aging process and the type of wood used for storage. The white colored version of the tequila, known as silver tequila or blanco, is the product obtained without or with very little additional aging. Tequila contains 38-55% alcohol making the beverage dangerous to drink because it has the ability to quickly intoxicate individuals. Mexican laws state that tequila can only be produced in the state of Jalisco and limited municipalities in the states of Guanajuato, Michoac√°n, Tamaulipas, and Nayarit. Tequila is recognized as a Mexican designation of origin product in more than 40 countries. Tequila is protected by the North American Free Trade Agreement in the United States and in Canada through bilateral agreements with individual countries such as Japan and Israel. Under United States law, Tequila is illegal to be sold in the U.S. at under 40% alcohol concentration.

      Unlike wine which contains tannins that can change over time, once tequila is bottled it does not change much. Since tequila is a distilled liquor, it does not require as strict of storage conditions as wine does, similarly whiskey, rum, and vodka do not strict storage conditions.
