←   Liqueur

Description:

      Liqueur is an alcoholic beverage which is produced from a distilled spirit that has been flavored with fruit, cream, herbs, flowers, spices, or nuts and bottled with added sugar or other sweeteners. The name liqueur is derived from the Latin word liquefacere which translates to “to make liquid”. Liqueurs are believed to be first commercially produced by medieval monks and alchemists. Liqueur has also been called, balms, crèmes, elixirs, and oils and have been used over the centuries as medicines and tonics, aphrodisiacs, and even love potions.

      Fruits liqueurs are created by the infusion method. In the method, fruit is steeped in the spirit which absorbs aroma, flavour, and colour. Plant liqueurs are naturally colorless and are produced by either percolation or distillation. They are also frequently colored with vegetable colorings. Liqueurs like many other spirits may be aged or bottled immediately.
