Media

      Mainstream media is often guilty of glamorizing alcohol use and often heavily encourages drinking to occur at social events. The alcohol industry spends billions of dollars each year on television, radio, print, and outdoor advertisements in attempt to perpetuate alcohol consumption. In 2002, the alcohol industry spent more than 990 million dollars only on T.V. One study found that the total number of alcohol advertisements on network, local, and cable television increased to 289,381 in 2002. Youth between the ages of 12 and 20 are likely to have seen 66,218 alcohol ads. Films such as the notable James Bond series often portray drinking as “classy” and “sexy”. These films attempt to convince their audience to associate alcohol with “having a good time”.


Technological/Medical


      The development of new alcohol rehab practices has had an immensely positive effect on people who suffer from alcohol dependence or alcoholism. Studies have shown that after six months, treatment for alcoholism is successful for approximately 40 to 70% of participants.

Culture

      Among teenagers, social media is often filled with pictures of adolescents drinking or being in the possession of alcohol. These images are often viewed as cool and may influence teens who do not regularly drink alcohol to consume it. Alcohol is often not thought of as a drug and has the perception of being socially acceptable which correlates with the fact that more than 80% of American youth consume alcohol before their 21st birthday.
