←   Gin

Description:

      Gin is a spirit which derives its predominant flavour from Juniperus communis, juniper berries. From its earliest origins in the Middle Ages, gin has evolved from a herbal medicine to an object of commerce in the spirits industry. Gin was named as a shortened form of the older English word genever. The European Union legally defines Gin as “a juniper-flavoured spirit made not via the redistillation of botanicals, but by simply adding approved natural flavouring substances to a neutral spirit of agricultural origin. The predominant flavour must be juniper.”

      Dutch physician Franciscus Sylvius is often credited with the creation of gin in the mid 17th century, however the existence of gin is much older. The earliest written reference to gin appears in the 13th century german encyclopaedic work Der Naturen Bloeme. Gin drinking in England rose significantly after the British government allowed unlicensed gin production at at the same time imposed a heavy tax on all imported spirits. Gin became incredibly cheap to purchase making the beverage quickly popular among the poor. At the time, of the 15,000 drinkings establishments in London half of them were shops for gin. Gin was also popular because of the reputation of beer being healthier and safer to drink than the unclean plain water.
