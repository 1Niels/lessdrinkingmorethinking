Think Before You Drink

How does drinking affect decision making?

17.6 million people suffer from alcohol abuse or dependence.

According to the National Council on Alcoholism and Drug Dependence in the United States alone 17.6 million people, or one in every 12 adults, suffers from alcohol dependence or abuse. Several million more engage in irresponsible, risky, and dangerous binge drinking patterns.

88,000 deaths are annually attributed to exessive alcohol use.

The NCADD states that alcoholism is the 3rd leading lifestyle-related cause of death in the nation. Excessive alcohol use is responsible for 2.5 million years of potential life lost anually which calculates to 28.4 years of potential life lost for each death.

1,400 college student deaths are linked to alcohol consumption.

Alcohol consumption by college students has been linked to at least 1,400 student death and 500,000 unintentional injuries every year. Alcohol has also been linked to a high proportion of deaths from falls, fires, and drownings.

27 people a day are killed in a drunk driving car crash.

In 2014 alone, alcohol-impaired driving fatalaties accounted for almost 10,000 deaths in the United States. The leading cause of death for teens are traffic accidents, 1/3 of these accidents involed alcohol or another substance. Adolescents who are involved with alcohol at a young age are 7 times more likely to be involved in an alcohol-related crash.

48% of 12th graders drank an alcoholic beverage in the past 30 days.

1/3 of all twelth graders reported to being drunk in the past 30 days. More than half of 12th graders reported having been drunk at least once in their life. Once study found that 31% of students in the study met criteria for a diagnosis of alcohol abuse and 6% for alcohol dependence in the past year.
