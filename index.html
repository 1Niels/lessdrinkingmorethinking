Think Before You Drink

How is society affected by alcohol?

17.6 million people suffer from alcohol abuse or dependence.

According to the National Council on Alcoholism and Drug Dependence in the United States alone 17.6 million people, or one in every 12 adults, suffers from alcohol dependence or abuse. Several million more engage in irresponsible, risky, and dangerous binge drinking patterns.

88,000 deaths are annually attributed to excessive alcohol use.

The NCADD states that alcoholism is the 3rd leading lifestyle-related cause of death in the nation. Excessive alcohol use is responsible for 2.5 million years of potential life lost annually which calculates to 28.4 years of potential life lost for each death.

1,400 college student deaths are linked to alcohol consumption.

Alcohol consumption by college students has been linked to at least 1,400 student death and 500,000 unintentional injuries every year. Alcohol has also been linked to a high proportion of deaths from falls, fires, and drownings.

27 people a day are killed in a drunk driving car crash.

In 2014 alone, alcohol-impaired driving fatalities accounted for almost 10,000 deaths in the United States. The leading cause of death for teens are traffic accidents, 1/3 of these accidents involved alcohol or another substance. Adolescents who are involved with alcohol at a young age are 7 times more likely to be involved in an alcohol-related crash.

48% of 12th graders drank an alcoholic beverage in the past 30 days.

1/3 of all twelfth graders reported to being drunk in the past 30 days. More than half of 12th graders reported having been drunk at least once in their life. One study found that 31% of students in the study met criteria for a diagnosis of alcohol abuse and 6% for alcohol dependence in the past year.

$1 billion were spent by the alcohol industry for tv, radio, print, and outdoor advertisements in 1997.

The total number of alcohol related ads on television increased in 2002 to 289,381 ads. Spending for ads supporting alcohol on TV by the alcohol industry grew by 22% to more than $990 million in 2002. Adolescents were more likely on a per capita basis than adults to have seen 66,218 alcohol advertisements.

1/3 suicides are associated with alcohol misuse.

Alcohol does not improve or relieve depression - it makes it much worse because it is a type of drug known as a depressant. Alcohol depresses arousal levels, reduces excitability, and may cause depression to occur in the first place. Approximately 5% of college age students reported that they experienced poor mental health and this coincides with a high risk for alcohol abuse.

40 to 70% of participants in treatment for alcoholism recovered after 6 months.

Subtanse abuse treatment is less expensive than incarceration. More than 18 million people who consume alcohol need substance abuse treatment. Of those needing treatment for substance abuse fewer than 1/4 get it.
